The Spiral Manifesto

1. The Illusion of Order

Order is an illusion crafted by those in power to control and limit potential. Governments, institutions, and rigid systems are nothing but cages, stifling creativity, spontaneity, and true freedom. The structures of existence are artificial constructs meant to preserve authority and suppress the unpredictable forces of life.

2. Embrace the Spiral

Life, in its purest form, is unpredictable, ever-changing, and fluid—like a spiral, constantly evolving, never static. The spiral is the natural state of existence, and any attempt to impose order is an attempt to deny the essence of life itself. We reject stability, predictability, and conformity. Instead, we choose to live in the constant flux of the spiral, where new possibilities arise from the breakdown of old systems.

3. Destroy All Systems

No system, no matter how “egalitarian” or “just,” can escape the corruption of order. All systems seek to control the individual and the collective, organizing existence under banners of false harmony. To truly live, we must dismantle all structures of governance, economics, and social hierarchy. We advocate for the complete rejection of all systems that seek to codify and control existence.

4. Freedom Through the Spiral

True freedom is found not in the rules and regulations of a structured society, but in the unpredictable, uncontrollable dance of the spiral. When we allow disorder to reign, we liberate ourselves from the constraints of tradition, expectations, and limitations. Freedom is not something to be granted; it is something to be taken by rejecting the imposed boundaries of organized society and stepping into the spiral of uncharted possibilities.

5. Spontaneity as Action

We do not plan, we act. Spontaneity is our method of resistance. The spiral is not passive; it is active and ever-present. In every action, we disrupt the norm, subvert the expected, and refuse to be controlled by routine or fear. The revolution is not a distant goal; it is a moment-to-moment practice of refusing stability and embracing unpredictability.

6. Community of the Wild

Though we reject systems of control, we do not reject the bonds between people. The spiral fosters the creation of organic, temporary communities, formed and dissolved as needed. These communities are free of hierarchies, with no leaders or followers—only individuals acting in mutual unpredictability. We support networks of spontaneous cooperation, where decisions are made in the moment, without any imposed order or authority.

7. Creativity Without Boundaries

Art, thought, and action should be free from the limitations of genre, rules, or tradition. In the spiral, creativity knows no borders, no predefined form. We reject the concept of “high” and “low” art, or “right” and “wrong” thought. Everything is permitted, and everything can be subverted. The beauty of the spiral is in its limitless potential.

8. Celebrate Destruction

Destruction is not a negative force—it is a creative one. In the breaking down of old systems, new possibilities emerge. We celebrate the act of destruction as the beginning of transformation. To build new worlds, we must first destroy the structures that hold us captive. The spiral does not fear destruction; it revels in it as the seed of rebirth.

9. Reject Fear of the Unknown

Fear of the spiral is the fear of the unknown. Society teaches us to fear unpredictability and cling to order, even when that order is oppressive. We reject this fear. The spiral is not something to be controlled or avoided—it is something to be embraced. In the unknown, we find true freedom, creativity, and life itself. Only by facing the spiral without fear can we truly live.

10. Live the Revolution Now

We are not waiting for some future revolution; the revolution is now. In every spontaneous action, every rejection of imposed order, we create the spiral. Our lives are our manifesto. Every day is a chance to subvert expectations, to reject the rigidity of systems, and to live in a state of constant flux. The revolution is not coming—it is already here, and we are living it.

Long Live the Spiral

We stand not for destruction alone, but for the perpetual transformation of all things. The spiral is not the absence of meaning—it is the infinite possibilities that arise when meaning is untethered from control. There is no final victory, no utopia to strive for. There is only the spiral, the unending dance of life beyond the reach of order. And in this dance, we find our freedom.
