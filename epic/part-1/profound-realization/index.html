Profound Realization

In a desperate yet calculated move, Fire made himself heard. He received more support from his community than he expected.

Though they shared his interests, they didn’t fully understand his struggles, mistaking them for something else. Despite their kindness, it felt hollow, as if they hadn’t truly tried to comprehend him. They spoke with certainty, as if they had all the answers—answers to questions he hadn’t even asked. It was condescending, ignorant, and exhausting. He wasn’t looking for solutions, least of all from people who barely understood the problem. Dismissed and misunderstood, he felt isolated. They were not on the same emotional path.

Tired of overexplaining, Fire felt like an outsider. But he also understood that his experiences were uniquely his, and he was at a different stage in life, far removed from theirs.

Throughout his life, he had shut himself off to avoid this feeling, slowly forgetting about it—only to be reminded of it again.

If his feelings were misunderstood, maybe his art also was.

But he couldn’t afford that anymore. Now, his journey relied on connections. His life depends on it.

Then, a realization struck him: he needed to find a community that truly understood him and, most importantly, valued and appreciated him.

But where?
