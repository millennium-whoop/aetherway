XMPP

I’ve wanted to use XMPP ever since I first learned about it a few years ago—a still-active relic from the 2000s, before social media and smartphones took over. Back then, this was how we communicated online: a small pop-up window on your computer displaying your contacts and their status—online, offline, busy, or away. That whole concept feels somewhat obsolete now, given our constant presence on the web. But I don’t think signaling our availability is pointless. Sometimes, we just want to chat.

Unfortunately, I couldn’t find anyone to chat with in a world that seems to have moved on, but the whole concept of NeoCities has given me hope.

Sparkle-sprinkle site? Circuits go full-on vibe-burst mode? Tell friends! More zoomy-zappy bouncy-boom, whoop whoop!
