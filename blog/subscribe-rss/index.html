The push to revive the ‘old web’—with platforms like NeoCities—brings back the late ’90s and early 2000s aesthetic. But pairing it with RSS feels anachronistic, as they come from different eras. A niche pre-Twitter technology, RSS never saw mainstream adoption.

Still, RSS offers a way to follow sites like social media—without algorithms. In an internet dominated by curated feeds and walled gardens, it remains a direct, decentralized alternative.
